  • Ospreys almost disappeared because they were being poisoned by the pesticide DDT.
  • They can live up to 20 years old.
  • Ospreys prefer to hunt in shallow waters but they can dive over 3 ft in water.
  • Raccoons often eat Osprey eggs while great horned owl will eat young chicks and sometimes even adult osprey.

General Info

The osprey tolerates a wide variety of habitats, nesting in any location near a body of water providing an adequate food supply. It is found on all continents except Antarctica, although in South America it occurs only as a non-breeding migrant. It is fish-eating bird of prey.

Osprey Description

Its toes are of equal length, its tarsi are reticulate, and its talons are rounded, rather than grooved. The osprey and owls are the only raptors whose outer toe is reversible, allowing them to grasp their prey with two toes in front and two behind. This is particularly helpful when they grab slippery fish.

Osprey Diet

The osprey is piscivorous, with fish making up 99% of its diet. Ospreys have vision that is well adapted to detecting underwater objects from the air.
