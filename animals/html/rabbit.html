Fun Facts

  • Rabbits perform an athletic leap, known as a ‘binky‘, when they’re happy
  • A rabbit’s teeth never stop growing! Instead, they’re gradually worn down as the rabbit chews on grasses, wildflowers and vegetables
  • Rabbits are very social creatures that live in groups. They live in warrens
  • Like cats, happy rabbits purr when they’re content and relaxed.
  • They can jump as high as 90 centimetres in one leap!

General Info

Rabbits, also known as bunnies or bunny rabbits, are small mammals. The European rabbit, which has been introduced on every continent except Antarctica, is familiar throughout the world as a wild prey animal and as a domesticated form of livestock and pet.

Bunnies around the world

Rabbits are ground dwellers that live in environments ranging from desert to tropical forest and wetland.Their natural geographic range in the Western Hemisphere encompasses the middle latitudes. In the Eastern Hemisphere rabbits are found in Europe, portions of Central and Southern Africa, the Indian subcontinent, Sumatra, and Japan. The European rabbit (Oryctolagus cuniculus) has been introduced to many locations around the world, and all breeds of domestic rabbit originate from the European. Nearly half of the world’s rabbit species are in danger of extinction; many are among the most vulnerable of all mammals.

Common Rabbit Misconceptions

Rabbits don't only eat carrots.Carrots actually aren’t that healthy for rabbits and should never be the only thing they eat. Hay should make up most of a pet rabbit’s daily diet. Carrots are high in carbohydrates, and eating too many may upset the rabbit’s digestive balance.
